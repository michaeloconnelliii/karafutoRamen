About us

We're passionate about quality Ramen. That means no shortcuts.

Hinata Yamamoto grew up eating Ramen in Onomichi (Hiroshima). At age 13, he started working at Tsubame, a small, rustic, Showa-era shop that specializes in Hiroshima-style Ramen (similar to a chicken-based shoyu Ramen). After working at Tsubame for 10 years, Hinata decided to move to the United States for a new adventure and change of scenery. He opened Kurafuto Ramen in March, 2009 and brought his take on Hiroshima-style Ramen to Chicago.

Our mission is to give you the best experience possible.

Kurafuto means "craft" in Japanese and the name is symbolic for the type of Ramen Hinata and his team create. Everything from the noodles to the tare are made in-house daily. The team works hard to give the people of Chicago the most delicious ramen in Illinois.
